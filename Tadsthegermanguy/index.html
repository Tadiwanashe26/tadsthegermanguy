What Are the German Levels?

The Common European Framework of Reference (CEFR) breaks language learning into 6 levels:

  • A1 – Beginner: Everyday phrases, greetings, and simple interaction.
  • A2 – Elementary: Describe daily life, work, shopping, and your surroundings.
  • B1 – Intermediate: Handle common situations, write simple texts, and express opinions.
  • B2 – Upper Intermediate: Understand complex ideas, speak fluently with native speakers.
  • C1 – Advanced: Use language flexibly and effectively in professional and academic settings.
  • C2 – Mastery: Understand virtually everything and express yourself spontaneously.
